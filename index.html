About Me

I am currently a Sports Administration major at Georgia State University and a Division I softball athlete. Being a student-athlete has played a huge role in shaping who I am today. Balancing school and athletics at this level has taught me how to stay disciplined, manage my time effectively, and stay focused even when things get overwhelming. Every day I am learning how to push myself, stay accountable, and continue improving both mentally and physically.

Growing up and playing sports for most of my life, I have always been competitive and driven, but being at the Division I level has taken that to another level. It has shown me what it really takes to succeed, not just talent, but consistency, preparation, and the ability to handle pressure. Whether it is early morning workouts, long practices, or balancing assignments with travel, these experiences have helped me build a strong work ethic that I will carry with me into my future career.

Through my major, I have also been able to see the bigger picture of sports beyond just playing. I have learned that there is a whole business side that keeps everything running, including management, marketing, operations, and leadership. This has really opened my eyes and made me realize that I want to stay involved in sports long-term, not just as an athlete, but as someone who helps organizations succeed behind the scenes.

One of the biggest things I have gained from my experience is leadership. Being part of a team has taught me how important communication, trust, and accountability are. I have learned how to support my teammates, step up when needed, and lead by example both on and off the field. These are skills that go far beyond sports and will help me in any career path I choose.

Overall, my goal is to continue growing both personally and professionally while staying connected to the world of sports. I want to take everything I have learned as an athlete and apply it to a future career in sports administration. Whether that is working with a team, an organization, or in a leadership role, I know that my experiences have prepared me to succeed and make an impact.
